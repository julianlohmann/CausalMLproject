# Literature Review

Approaches or solutions that have been tried before on similar projects.

**Summary of Each Work**:

- **Source 1**: [The effect of pre-primary education on primary school performance]

  - **[Link](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpubeco.2008.09.002)**
  - **Objective**: Estimating the causal effect of pre-primary education on (third class) primary school performance
  - **Methods**: Regression Model
  - **Outcomes**: one year of pre-primary school increases average third grade test scores by 8% of a mean or by 23% of the standard deviation of the distribution of test scores

- **Source 2**: [Effect of the Duration of Preschool Attendance on Academic Achievements—Evidence from PISA 2018]

  - **[Link](https://doi.org/10.1007/s12187-023-10095-1)**
  - **Objective**: Estimating the causal effect of amount of pre-primary education in years (continuous treatment) on PISA test score performance
  - **Methods**: Generalized propensity score weighting based on PISA 2018 data from multiple countries
  - **Outcomes**: inverted U-shaped relationship between the duration of preschool attendance and academic achievements (optimal duration between 2 and 4 years)


- **Source 3**: [Pre-primary education and longterm education performance: Evidence from Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) Thailand]

  - **[Link](https://doi.org/10.1177/1476718X15616834)**
  - **Objective**: Testing the long-term effect of pre-primary education on education performance in PISA
  - **Methods**: Regression approach (econometric model) using PISA 2009 and 2012 data from Thailand
  - **Outcomes**: Pre-primary education had a significant and positive association with cognitive performance in all three areas of literacy tested under PISA
